{ tutorial }

The rules are easy. You are bidding on the NEXT round's pot. If the sum of your bids is largest, you get the NEXT (not the one your bids went in) pot. You can place as many bids as you like and all your bids are added up. To place your bids you have a limited time (by default 666 blocks). This limited bidding time is called "roundtime". The roundtime is measured in blocks ('cause we are on the blockchain, jeah!) - no seconds, minutes or hours.

Please note, that it takes some time until you bid is in the blockchain and thus valid! Pending bids are NOT valid!

Also, there is a low chance that the blockchain change it states!

There are different versions of the game. They just differ in the round time. You can change the roundtime in your dashboard at the bottom (here).

Ok, here is an example:

You bid 12ETH. For now your bid is the highest. In the same round someone bids 13ETH. Now the other guy is the leader. To be the leader again you just need to bid more than 1ETH (because your bids are added up. Thus, e.g. 12ETH+1.1ETH). If you bid the same amount, the other guy will still be the leader. You need to bid more - not the same. Ok, let's say the sum of you bid was the largest in in round 6. Thus, you are the owner of the contract's pot in round 7. You do not need to do anything. Well, you can still bid on this pot to raise the total pot's size. Let's assume in round 7 there are 800 accounts bidding on average 6ETH. Thus, the pot you own is about 800 * 6ETH=4800ETH (There is a small comission kept back). To get your ether, just press the black withdraw button here.

If your bid is not the highest or someone places a higher bid than yours, your ether remains in the pot. But you can bid as often as you want and your bids are added up. If you place bid (pending) but your bid ends up in a block after the round time ended, your bid will go into the next round's pot.

What do I need to participate?

Just install MetaMask and enjoy - you will see black buttons to play the game if you are logged into MetaMask.

{ faq }

  • What is the "round time" and how can I cange it?

    Roundtime is how many blocks one round lasts. You can choose you favorite roundtime on top of the page. Here is an overview which roundtimes are currently online. In case you are looking for a specific roundtime. Post it in our social networks. Thanks and enjoy! :)

  • How do I withdraw ether?

    We highly recommend using a web3 injected browser like MIST or your favorite Browser with MetaMask! If you are logged in, you should see a black "withdraw" button on this page. If you bought a pot you can withdraw it there.

    If you do not have a web3 injected browser. The smart contract has a specific funtion called "payDay". You can send 0ETH to this function using your favorite software.

  • I can not see any black buttons (like bid or withdraw) on the page!

    Most likley you do not use MIST or the MetaMask extension or you are not logged in. Please check if you have an ethereum (main network) account and if you are logged in. If you still do not see the buttons please post a bug report in one of our social networks. If everything failes, check if you can use the functions "luckyChance" or "payDay" in your favorite wallet.

  • Do the bids I send add up?

    Yes. The bids add up. The sum of all your bids are called stack. The leader is the one with the biggest stack.

  • How much time do I have to withdraw my funds?

    Plenty. You have around 120 days to withdraw your pot. After this period the pot is cleared to prevent huge amounts of deadlocked ether.

  • What if two bids are qual?

    First comes, first serves. The first bid gets the lead. You need to bid more and not the same.

  • Why do you keep a small fee?

    It took over a month to set everything up and we are still improving, maintaining and debugging. We would love to live from love and air but - well, you know... Anyway, the fee is also used to stimulate the pot. Thus, we pump back ether to make pots bigger in times where less people are bidding. The fee is really fair (9.2%). You can check everything, if you watch the smart contract on etherscan. It's verfied there. We cannot hide anything nor change it. This smart contract is really fair and fun.

  • I placed a bid and I can not see it!

    On the blockchain in can happen, that transactions are not mined or need a long (VERY LONG) time to be mined. Therefore it can also happen that leaders can change after a round ends. That happens if blocks become invalid and replaced. If you do not understand how a blockchain works, go and make your homework.

  • Why does the "blocks left in this round" and the round counter do not change?

    If noone is playing the game, the round will stay the same and the countdown does not start. As soon as you place a bid, you will open up a new round! Try it!

  • The pot size and the leading bid have an "e" like "1e-7ETH" in the number. What is that?

    That is just another way to say how many leading 0 the number has. The "e" stands for the "exponend".
